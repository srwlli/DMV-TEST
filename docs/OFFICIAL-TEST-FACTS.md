in specificity, that is noted. This is the canonical reference for the app's
test-structure claims.

## The 20 / 20 split — what the law actually says

OAC 4501:1-1-08 defines the written knowledge test in **two written parts**
(plus a vision/physical screening):

- **Part — Motor Vehicle Laws:** covers "motor vehicle laws, rules of the road
  pertaining to safety, regulations of the bureau of motor vehicles … and
  guidelines established by the federal motor carrier safety administration."
  **A minimum of twenty questions, equally weighted;** pass = 75%.
- **Part — Traffic Signs:** covers "highway warnings and traffic signs,
  signaling devices and directions given in the English language."
  **A minimum of twenty questions, equally weighted;** pass = 75%.

**20 + 20 = 40**, matching the manual's "40 multiple-choice questions."

### Precision note (do not overstate)
The rule specifies 75% **for each written part** and describes them as separate
parts. It uses "a minimum of twenty" — so 20 is the floor, not a hard cap. The
rule does **not** contain explicit wording like "the parts are scored
independently and you must pass each separately." The per-part 75% is stated
per part in the regulation; the manual states a single "75% passage rate" for
the 40-question test overall. Both are true as quoted. The app treats the test
as **20 signs + 20 rules, 75% overall (30/40)**, and additionally reports each
section's score so a user can see per-part performance — without asserting a
legal "must pass each part separately" claim the source doesn't explicitly make.
